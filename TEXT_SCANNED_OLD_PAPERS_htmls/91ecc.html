                   SOME  CONNECTIONS  BETWEEN  CHAOTIC
            DYNAMICAL  SYSTEMS  AND  CONTROL  SYSTEMS*

                                       Francesca ALBERTINI, Eduardo D. SONTAG
                                     SYCON- Rutgers Center for Systems and Control
                      Department of Mathematics, Rutgers University, New Brunswick, NJ 08903


Abstract.  This paper shows how to extend recent results of Colonius and Kliemann, regarding connections
between chaos and controllability, from continuous to discrete time.  The extension is nontrivial because the
results all rely on basic properties of the accessibility Lie algebra which fail to hold in discrete time. Thus, this
paper first develops further results in nonlinear accessibility, and then shows how a theorem can be proved,
which while analogous to the one given in the work by Colonius and Klieman, also exhibits some important
differences. A counterexample is used to show that the theorem given in continuous time cannot be generalized
in a straightforward manner.
Keywords. Discrete-time, chaotic systems, Lie algebras, nonlinear systems, controllability, acessibility.
