  1. D. Jatkar, M. Ali Al-Radhawi, C.A. Voigt, and E.D. Sontag. A modeling framework for optimizing CRISPRi genetic logic circuits. In Proc. 64th IEEE Conference on Decision and Control (CDC), 2025. Note: Submitted. Keyword(s): biomolecular systems, systems biology, synthetic biology, CIRSPRi.
    Abstract:
    The goal of designing biochemical circuits in synthetic biology is to make modular and tractable systems that perform well with predictable behaviors. Developing formalisms towards the design of such systems has proven to be difficult due to the diverse retroactive effects that appear with respect to the context of the cell. Repressor-based circuits have various applications in biosynthesis, therapeutics, and bioremediation. Particularly using CRISPRi, competition for components of the system (unbound dCas9) can affect the achievable dynamic range of repression. Moreover, the toxicity of dCas9 via non-specific binding inhibits high levels of expression and limits the performance of genetic circuits. In this work, we study the computation of Boolean functions through CRISPRi based circuits built out of NOT and NOR gates. We provide algebraic expressions that allow us to evaluate the steady-state behaviors of any realized circuit. Further, we find that under the condition of linear competition between promoters for dCas9, certain circuit architectures perform better than others depending on factors such as circuit depth, fan-in, and fan-out. This framework provides a mathematical template for evaluating the performance of repressor-based circuits.
