  1. E.D. Sontag, D. Biswas, N.J. Cowan, and ag. An observability result related to active sensing. In 22nd IFAC World Congress, IFAC-PapersOnLine, volume 56, pages 60-64, 2022. Note: ArXiv 2210.03848. [PDF] [doi:https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ifacol.2023.10.1408] Keyword(s): linear systems, nonlinear systems, observables, Koopman embedding, duality, nonlinear systems, observability, active sensing.
    Abstract:
    For a general class of translationally invariant systems with a specific category of nonlinearity in the output, this paper presents necessary and sufficient conditions for global observability. Critically, this class of systems cannot be stabilized to an isolated equilibrium point by dynamic output feedback. These analyses may help explain the active sensing movements made by animals when they perform certain motor behaviors, despite the fact that these active sensing movements appear to run counter to the primary motor goals. The findings presented here establish that active sensing underlies the maintenance of observability for such biological systems, which are inherently nonlinear due to the presence of the high-pass sensor dynamics.embeddings".} @techreport{2022_sontag_biswas_cowan_arxiv
